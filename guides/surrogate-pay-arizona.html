FAQ

Surrogate FAQ: Arizona

Is surrogacy legal in Arizona?

Yes, gestational surrogacy is practiced in Arizona. A reproductive attorney will be involved to ensure your contract is legally sound before proceeding.

Do I have to live in Arizona to be a surrogate here?

Typically yes. Most agencies require you to live in the state where you carry the pregnancy, as medical care and legal jurisdiction are state-specific.

Is surrogate pay taxable in Arizona?

Base compensation is generally taxable at the federal level. Expense reimbursements (travel, maternity clothes) are typically not taxable. Consult a tax professional for Arizona-specific guidance.

How long does surrogacy take in Arizona?

18–24 months from application to delivery: matching (1–6 months), medical screening, legal review, and the full pregnancy term.

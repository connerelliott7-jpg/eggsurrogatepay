🏥 Not Medical Advice

Nothing on this website constitutes medical advice. Egg donation and surrogacy are medical procedures that carry real risks, including but not limited to:

  • Ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS) in egg donors
  • Risks associated with hormonal medications
  • Risks of pregnancy, labor, and delivery in surrogates
  • Psychological impacts of the egg donation or surrogacy process

Always consult a licensed medical professional before making any decisions about egg donation or surrogacy. The health information on this site is for general informational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

⚖️ Not Legal Advice — Laws Vary by State

Surrogacy and egg donation are governed by a complex patchwork of state and federal laws that vary significantly across the United States:

  • Some states have explicit statutes permitting or regulating compensated surrogacy
  • Other states have no surrogacy-specific laws, creating legal ambiguity
  • A small number of states have laws that restrict or complicate compensated surrogacy arrangements
  • Parentage laws, pre-birth orders, and contract enforceability vary by jurisdiction

Nothing on this site constitutes legal advice. Consult a licensed reproductive attorney in your state before entering into any surrogacy or egg donation arrangement. The legal landscape changes frequently.
